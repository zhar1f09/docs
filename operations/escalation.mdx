Not every ticket can or should be resolved by the Operations team alone. Knowing when to escalate — and how to do it properly — is one of the most important skills in this role.

## When to Escalate

Escalate a ticket to HR when:

- The customer is asking for a refund or raising a payment dispute.
- There is a possible policy violation or misconduct to report.
- The issue is outside your knowledge or authority to resolve.
- The customer is escalating in frustration and the situation risks becoming worse.
- You are unsure whether you are handling something correctly.

<Tip>
It is always better to escalate and get guidance than to handle something incorrectly. Do not be afraid to ask for help.
</Tip>

## How to Escalate

<Steps>
  <Step title="Notify HR">
    Inform an available HR representative about the ticket. Provide the ticket details, a brief summary of the situation, and why you are escalating.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Do Not Close the Ticket">
    Keep the ticket open until HR or a senior staff member has taken over and confirmed it is resolved.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stay Available">
    Remain available for any follow-up questions from HR. You may be asked to provide more context about the interaction.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## After Escalation

Once HR has taken over, step back and let them handle the situation. Do not continue engaging with the customer on the escalated issue unless specifically asked to.

<Note>
Escalating is not a sign of failure — it is the right call. The Operations team exists to handle what it can and to route what it cannot to the right people.
</Note>
