As part of the Operations team, your main responsibility is handling customer support tickets. This page walks through how to do that well.

## Your Role in a Ticket

When a customer opens a support ticket, your job is to:

- Understand what the customer needs.
- Provide helpful, accurate information.
- Maintain a professional and respectful tone throughout.
- Resolve the issue if it falls within your scope — or escalate if it does not.

## Handling a Ticket

<Steps>
  <Step title="Acknowledge the Customer">
    When you pick up a ticket, greet the customer and let them know you are there to help. A quick, professional response sets the right tone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Understand the Request">
    Read the customer's message carefully. Ask clarifying questions if needed before offering a solution.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Provide Assistance">
    Help the customer with their request. Keep your responses clear and concise. If you can resolve the issue, do so.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close or Escalate">
    Once the customer's issue is resolved, close the ticket. If the issue is beyond your scope, escalate it — see the [Escalation guide](/operations/escalation).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Standards to Uphold

- **Always be professional and respectful**, regardless of how the customer behaves.
- **Do not make promises** you cannot keep or that are outside your authority.
- **Do not handle order or payment issues** — redirect those to the appropriate team.

<Warning>
Do not attempt to handle disputes, refund requests, or policy violations on your own. These must be escalated to HR.
</Warning>
